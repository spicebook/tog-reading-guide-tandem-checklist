Throne of Glass
Reading Order
Throne of Glass by Sarah J. Maas is a complete epic fantasy series filled with assassins, ancient magic, found family, political intrigue, and slow-burn romance. With its expanding world and interconnected storylines, it’s fast-paced, emotional, and incredibly easy to get lost in.

If you’re starting the series—or returning for a reread—you’ll quickly discover that there isn’t just one way to read Throne of Glass. Because of the way the prequel novella and later books are structured, readers generally choose between two popular reading orders, each offering a slightly different experience.

Below, you’ll find both options so you can pick the one that best fits your reading style.
Recommended
Chronological

Recommended Reading Order

This is the reading order most commonly recommended by fans for the strongest emotional payoff. In this version, The Assassin’s Blade is placed after Crown of Midnight, where its stories add depth, heartbreak, and context right before the series expands in Heir of Fire. This placement allows you to connect more deeply with characters, relationships, and future events. This order also allows for a tandem read of Empire of Storms and Tower of Dawn, creating a smoother narrative bridge into the final book, Kingdom of Ash.

1. Throne of Glass
2. Crown of Midnight
3. The Assassin's Blade
4. Heir of Fire
5. Queen of Shadows
6. Empire of Storms
7. Tower of Dawn
8. Kingdom of Ash

Chronological Reading Order

The chronological reading order follows the story strictly by in-world timeline. In this version, The Assassin’s Blade is read first as a true prequel, since the novella takes place before the events of Throne of Glass. This approach gives you early context for Celaena’s past and introduces characters and locations that reappear later in the series.

1. The Assassin's Blade
2. Throne of Glass
3. Crown of Midnight
4. Heir of Fire
5. Queen of Shadows
6. Empire of Storms
7. Tower of Dawn
8. Kingdom of Ash
